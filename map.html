About this map
Introduction
Anthropogenic changes to the Earth's biosphere are now so advanced that they threaten the favorable conditions the Earth provides for our species. Here we propose a set of minimal actions that need to be taken in order to maintain a biosphere that will enable our species to persist into the future. The essence of these actions is to increase the level of protection for wild life and wild places several orders of magnitude and to stop poisoning our ecosystems. The result will be a balance of human and natural ecosystems, resilient in the face of disturbance and change and providing productive resources and sanctuaries for life.
Map layers
The maps shown here depict two classes of terrestrial and marine areas that we argue must be managed in a way that allows the favorable conditions the Earth provides for our species to persist. We call these two classes "managed more natural", and "managed more exploited". The terrrestrial layers that make up the "managed more natural" class include: protected areas (existing and proposed, all IUCN categories), remaining habitat, wilderness areas, major rivers, wetlands and ice caps. Marine layers in the "managed more natural" class include existing protected areas, mangroves, and proposed "high-seas" protected areas.
Data sources
